Field Marshal Sam Manekshaw

The fearless voice of the Indian Army — a legend, a leader, a patriot.

Here's a time line of Sam Manekshaw :

  • 1914 - Born on 3 April in Amritsar, Punjab, British India.
  • 1932 - Joins the first batch of the Indian Military Academy (IMA), Dehradun.
  • 1934 - Commissioned into the British Indian Army, posted to the Frontier Force Regiment.
  • 1942 - Severely wounded during World War II in Burma (Myanmar). Awarded the Military Cross for bravery.
  • 1947 - Serves during the Partition and Indo-Pakistani War of 1947–48.
  • 1952–1962 - Rises through key military positions, serving in staff and command roles during a crucial phase of Indian Army modernization.
  • 1962 - Appointed Corps Commander during the Sino-Indian War.
  • 1965 - Plays a key strategic role in the Indo-Pakistani War of 1965.
  • 1969 - Appointed Chief of the Army Staff of India.
  • 1971 -Leads India to historic victory in the Indo-Pakistani War of 1971, resulting in the creation of Bangladesh.
  • 1973 -Becomes the first Indian Army officer to be promoted to the rank of Field Marshal.
  • 2008 - Passes away on 27 June in Wellington, Tamil Nadu, India.
